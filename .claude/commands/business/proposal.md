## Proposal Structure

1. **Executive Summary** — one paragraph framing the client's challenge and proposed solution
2. **Understanding of the Problem** — demonstrate understanding of their specific situation
3. **Proposed Solution** — what will be done, broken into phases or workstreams
4. **Deliverables** — specific, tangible outputs with acceptance criteria
5. **Timeline** — phases with milestones (avoid specific dates unless provided)
6. **Investment** — pricing structure (leave amounts as placeholders unless specified)
7. **About Anundio** — brief positioning statement highlighting relevant expertise
8. **Next Steps** — clear call to action

## Standards

- Write in professional but approachable tone
- Lead with **business value**, not technical details
- Be **specific about deliverables** — avoid vague language
- Include **assumptions and exclusions** to protect scope
- Frame pricing as **investment**, not cost
- Keep it concise — decision-makers don't read 20-page proposals
- Use Swedish business conventions where appropriate (but write in English unless specified)
