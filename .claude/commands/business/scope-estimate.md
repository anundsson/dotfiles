## Analysis Framework

### 1. Scope Definition
- **In Scope**: What will be delivered
- **Out of Scope**: What is explicitly excluded
- **Assumptions**: What must be true for the estimate to hold
- **Dependencies**: What the client must provide or decide

### 2. Work Breakdown
Break the project into workstreams, each with:
- Description of work
- Deliverables
- Effort range (optimistic / realistic / pessimistic)
- Dependencies on other workstreams

### 3. Risk Assessment
- Identify key risks that could affect scope or effort
- Rate likelihood and impact
- Define mitigation strategies

### 4. Effort Summary
- Total effort range across all workstreams
- Recommended pricing approach (fixed, T&M, retainer, milestone-based)
- Suggested payment schedule

## Standards

- Use **effort ranges** (e.g., 3-5 days), never single-point estimates
- Be explicit about **assumptions** — they're the most common source of scope creep
- Include a **buffer** recommendation (typically 15-25% for unknowns)
- Consider the **solo consultant** context — no parallelism across people
- Flag areas where **discovery work** is needed before committing to scope

## Output

Generate a structured scope document suitable for internal planning or as an appendix to a client proposal.
